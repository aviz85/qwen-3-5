A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost?
The ball costs $0.05. Here is the breakdown: If the bat costs $1.00 more than the ball, and they total $1.10, you can set up two equations: Bat + Ball = $1.10 and Bat - Ball = $1.00. Key Insights & Patterns

🔥
The Overthinking Trap
The poetry_ai_haiku result is the most revealing: 11,592 tokens of reasoning for a 12-word output. The model's chain-of-thought mode (enabled by default in Qwen3.5) can create runaway internal deliberation when the task involves multiple simultaneous constraints (syllable count + emotion + irony). The output was excellent — but at 200× the token cost of an efficient response.
🧠
The 4b Intelligence Threshold
The blind spot riddle reveals a clear capability cliff between 2b and 4b. Below 4b, models produce anatomically plausible but wrong answers ("the lens", "the eye itself"). At 4b and above, models identify the optic disc correctly. This suggests that spatial/anatomical reasoning requires a minimum parameter count — more tokens aren't enough, you need more weights.
⚡
Speed vs Intelligence Trade-off
0.8b runs at 33 t/s — the fastest by far — but answers incorrectly. 27b runs at 5.1 t/s with near-perfect accuracy. The 4b sweet spot: 24.8 t/s on M5 Metal, correct on most tasks. For production use cases, 4b offers the best intelligence-per-second ratio on consumer hardware.
🌐
Multilingual Competence
The Hebrew hebrew_heisenberg prompt was answered correctly in Hebrew — technically accurate explanations calibrated for two very different audiences. The model didn't switch to English or produce awkward transliterations. However, it used 6,217 tokens for 2 sentences, suggesting multilingual reasoning adds significant computational overhead.
⏱
Timeout Pattern Analysis
All 5 timeouts share a common structure: hard constraint + verification loop. The model must generate a candidate, verify it against multiple rules, reject it, and retry — indefinitely. Logic puzzles requiring mutual-knowledge reasoning (hats) and impossible letter constraints (no 'e' in elephant facts) are especially prone to this. Solution: add "think": false for constrained tasks.
🎯
Where It Truly Excels
The model shines brightest on: philosophical analysis (philosophy_identity), structured explanations (science_sky), causal counterfactual reasoning (history_counterfactual), and creative synthesis (synthesis_final). These tasks play to transformer strengths — pattern matching, analogical reasoning, and structured generation. Math trap avoidance (math_trap) also impressive.

Methodology — Creative Seeds

The Problem with Standard Benchmarks
Standard AI benchmarks suffer from a critical flaw: models are trained on data that includes benchmark solutions. MMLU, HumanEval, GSM8K — these are well-known enough that high scores may reflect memorization rather than reasoning.

This evaluation used the Creative Seeds technique: 50 absurdist micro-stories used as cognitive disruptors before generating prompts. By "contaminating" the prompt-generation process with nonsense, the resulting riddles are structurally novel — unlikely to appear in training data.
Example seed used: "A quilt made entirely of borrowed time unravels when anyone nearby tells a true story about the future."
Evaluation Philosophy
Rather than multiple-choice answers, all prompts require open-ended generation, making it harder to "guess correctly." The prompts test:

Reasoning — Can the model resist cognitive biases?
Creativity — Does it generate genuinely novel content?
Constraint following — Can it hold multiple rules simultaneously?
Self-awareness — Is it honest about its own limitations?
Synthesis — Can it connect disparate domains?

The blind spot riddle was specifically crafted to avoid the common AI pattern of answering "the iris" or "the pupil" — which correct riddles often do. The optic disc formulation forces genuine anatomical recall, not pattern matching on common riddle formats.
